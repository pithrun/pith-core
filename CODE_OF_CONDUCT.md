# Code of Conduct

## Our Standards

Pith is a technical project. We expect contributors to engage honestly, charitably, and directly.

Specifically:

- **Be direct.** Say what you mean. Disagreement about technical decisions is healthy and expected — argue the idea, not the person.
- **Be accurate.** Don't make claims you haven't verified. If you're uncertain, say so.
- **Be constructive.** Bug reports, criticism, and pushback are all welcome when they come with specifics. Vague negativity isn't useful to anyone.
- **Respect people's time.** Read existing issues and documentation before opening new threads. Keep discussions on-topic.

## Not acceptable

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language of any kind
- Deliberate intimidation or sustained disruption of discussion
- Publishing others' private information without consent
- Spam, self-promotion unrelated to the project, or off-topic solicitation

## Scope

This applies in all project spaces — GitHub Issues, PRs, Discussions, and any official communication channels. It also applies when someone is representing the project publicly.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior can be reported to **conduct@pith.run**. All reports will be reviewed promptly and handled confidentially.

Violations may result in temporary or permanent removal from project spaces at the maintainers' discretion.
